Air gap

A security measure that isolates a computer or network from other networks, particularly the internet.

Description

An air gap is a cybersecurity strategy that involves physically isolating a computer or network from unsecured networks, especially the internet. This means that no direct connections exist between the air-gapped system and external networks, which significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access, data breaches, and cyber attacks. Air-gapped systems are often used in environments that require high security, such as military networks, critical infrastructure, and sensitive data storage. While air gaps provide a robust layer of security, they can also pose challenges for operational efficiency and data transfer, as any necessary updates or data sharing must be conducted manually via physical media, such as USB drives. This method of isolation effectively creates a barrier against remote attacks, but it does not eliminate all risks, as physical access or compromised removable media can still introduce vulnerabilities. Organizations utilizing air gaps must implement strict protocols and monitoring to ensure that the physical boundaries are maintained and that any data transfer is conducted securely.

Examples

  • Military systems that are air-gapped to protect sensitive information from cyber espionage.
  • Industrial control systems (ICS) in critical infrastructure that are isolated to prevent cyber attacks.

Additional Information

  • Air gaps are not foolproof; insider threats and physical access can still compromise security.
  • Regular audits and strict access controls are essential for maintaining the integrity of air-gapped systems.
