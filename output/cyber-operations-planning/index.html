Cyber operations planning

The process of preparing and coordinating cyber operations to achieve specific objectives.

Description

Cyber operations planning refers to the systematic approach taken by organizations, especially within military and defense sectors, to prepare for and execute cyber operations. This involves assessing the current cyber threat landscape, defining clear operational objectives, and developing strategies to mitigate risks while maximizing the effectiveness of cyber capabilities. The planning phase includes identifying critical assets, understanding potential adversaries' capabilities, and formulating tactics that align with broader organizational goals. It encompasses both offensive and defensive operations, ensuring that the organization can protect its assets while also being prepared to respond to or preempt threats. Effective cyber operations planning requires collaboration across various teams, including IT, security, legal, and compliance, to ensure a comprehensive understanding of the operational environment. Moreover, it is essential to incorporate continuous monitoring and assessment to adapt plans in response to evolving cyber threats.

Examples

  • A military organization devising a cyber strategy to disrupt an adversary's communications during a conflict.
  • A corporation planning a defensive operation to strengthen its cybersecurity posture against potential data breaches.

Additional Information

  • Cyber operations planning often incorporates lessons learned from previous incidents.
  • It requires ongoing training and exercises to ensure readiness for real-world cyber scenarios.
