Integrity

Integrity refers to the assurance that information is accurate, reliable, and protected from unauthorized modification.

Description

In cybersecurity, integrity is a fundamental principle that ensures that data remains unchanged and uncorrupted during storage, transmission, and processing. It is crucial for maintaining the trustworthiness of information. Integrity can be compromised through various means, such as unauthorized access, malware attacks, or human error. Mechanisms to ensure integrity include checksums, hashes, and digital signatures, which help verify that the data has not been altered in an unauthorized manner. For example, when a file is transmitted over the internet, its hash value may be calculated and sent along with it. The recipient can then calculate the hash value of the received file and compare it to the original hash to confirm that the file has not been tampered with. Ensuring data integrity is essential for protecting sensitive information and maintaining compliance with regulations. Failure to uphold data integrity can lead to serious consequences, including data loss, breaches of confidentiality, and damage to an organization’s reputation.

Examples

  • Using checksums to verify the integrity of downloaded software files.
  • Implementing access controls to prevent unauthorized changes to sensitive data.

Additional Information

  • Integrity is one of the three pillars of the CIA triad, alongside Confidentiality and Availability.
  • Data integrity can be enforced through both technical controls and organizational policies.
