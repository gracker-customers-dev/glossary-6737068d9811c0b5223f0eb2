Macro virus

A type of malware that uses the macro programming language in applications to automate tasks and can replicate itself.

Description

Macro viruses are a specific class of computer viruses that are written in the macro programming language used by applications such as Microsoft Word and Excel. These viruses exploit the macro functionality, which allows users to automate repetitive tasks in documents and spreadsheets. When a user opens an infected document, the macro virus can automatically execute its code, potentially infecting the user's system or spreading to other documents. Macro viruses can perform a variety of malicious actions, including corrupting files, stealing sensitive information, or propagating themselves by sending infected documents via email. Due to their reliance on user interaction (such as enabling macros), macro viruses are often delivered through phishing emails or malicious attachments. While the prevalence of macro viruses has decreased with the rise of other forms of malware, they remain a concern, particularly in environments where users are less aware of cybersecurity best practices. Therefore, users should be cautious about enabling macros in documents from untrusted sources.

Examples

  • Concept virus: A macro virus that spreads through infected Word documents.
  • Melissa virus: One of the first widespread macro viruses that used email to propagate.

Additional Information

  • Macro viruses can be mitigated by disabling macros by default in applications.
  • Regular software updates and antivirus solutions can help protect against macro viruses.
