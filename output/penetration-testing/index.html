Penetration testing

A simulated cyber attack on a system to identify vulnerabilities.

Description

Penetration testing, often referred to as pen testing, is a critical component of cybersecurity that involves simulating real-world attacks on computer systems, networks, or web applications to evaluate their security posture. The primary goal of penetration testing is to identify vulnerabilities that could be exploited by malicious actors, thereby providing organizations with a clear understanding of their security weaknesses. Pen tests can be conducted manually or with automated tools and are typically performed by skilled security professionals known as ethical hackers. The testing process often includes planning, reconnaissance, gaining access, maintaining access, and analysis/reporting. By conducting regular penetration tests, organizations can proactively mitigate risks, enhance their security measures, and comply with regulatory requirements. It is an essential practice for organizations seeking to protect sensitive data and maintain the integrity of their systems against evolving cyber threats.

Examples

  • Testing a web application for SQL injection vulnerabilities.
  • Simulating a phishing attack to assess employee awareness and response.

Additional Information

  • Penetration tests can be categorized into black box, white box, and gray box testing.
  • They are often part of a broader security assessment strategy that includes vulnerability scanning and security audits.
