Personal identifying information

Information that can be used to identify an individual.

Description

Personal Identifying Information (PII) refers to any data that could potentially identify a specific individual. This includes a wide range of information that, when combined, can uniquely distinguish one person from another. PII is a critical concern in the field of cybersecurity because its unauthorized access or disclosure can lead to identity theft, fraud, and other malicious activities. Common examples of PII include names, addresses, social security numbers, and financial information. Organizations that collect, store, or process PII are subject to various legal and regulatory frameworks that dictate how this data should be protected. The importance of safeguarding PII has increased with the rise of data breaches and cyberattacks that target sensitive personal information. Proper handling of PII not only helps in maintaining individual privacy but is also essential for compliance with laws such as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA). Thus, understanding what constitutes PII and implementing robust security measures to protect it is fundamental in today’s digital landscape.

Examples

  • Social Security Number (SSN)
  • Email addresses

Additional Information

  • PII can be categorized as sensitive or non-sensitive, with sensitive PII requiring more stringent protection measures.
  • Organizations should implement data encryption, access controls, and regular audits to protect PII.
