Phishing

A cyber attack that uses deceptive techniques to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information.

Description

Phishing is a type of cyber attack that involves the use of fraudulent communications, typically emails, that appear to come from reputable sources. The primary goal of phishing is to steal sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, credit card numbers, and other personal details. Attackers often create a sense of urgency or fear, prompting recipients to click on malicious links or download infected attachments. These communications may mimic legitimate organizations, including banks, online services, or social media platforms, making it difficult for individuals to discern their authenticity. Phishing attacks can take various forms, including spear phishing (targeted attacks on specific individuals) and whaling (targeting high-profile executives). With the increasing sophistication of these attacks, it is crucial for users to remain vigilant, verify sources, and implement security measures such as multi-factor authentication to protect themselves from falling victim to these scams.

Examples

  • An email that appears to be from a bank asking you to verify your account information by clicking a link.
  • A message on social media that claims you've won a prize and asks for your personal details to claim it.

Additional Information

  • Phishing can also occur through SMS (known as smishing) and voice calls (known as vishing).
  • Organizations often conduct phishing simulations to educate employees about recognizing and responding to phishing attempts.
