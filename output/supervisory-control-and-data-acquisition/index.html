Supervisory control and data acquisition

A system that gathers and analyzes real-time data to control industrial processes.

Description

Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition (SCADA) refers to a system used for remote monitoring and control of physical processes, such as those in industrial, infrastructure, and facility-based processes. SCADA systems are crucial for managing operations across various sectors, including water treatment, oil and gas, electricity, and manufacturing. These systems collect data from sensors and devices at remote sites, transmitting this information back to a central control system. Operators can then monitor the data, visualize the system status, and control the processes in real-time. However, SCADA systems have become increasingly targeted in cybersecurity incidents due to their critical role in infrastructure. Cyber threats to SCADA systems can lead to operational disruptions, safety hazards, and data breaches. Therefore, understanding the security implications of SCADA systems and implementing robust cybersecurity measures is essential for protecting these vital assets from unauthorized access and malicious attacks.

Examples

  • Water treatment plant SCADA system monitoring chemical levels and flow rates.
  • Electricity grid SCADA system managing power distribution and outage detection.

Additional Information

  • SCADA systems often utilize protocols such as Modbus, DNP3, and IEC 61850.
  • Cybersecurity measures for SCADA include network segmentation, encryption, and access controls.
