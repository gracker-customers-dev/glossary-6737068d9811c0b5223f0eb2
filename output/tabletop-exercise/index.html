Tabletop exercise

A simulated, discussion-based exercise to evaluate and improve response plans in cybersecurity.

Description

A tabletop exercise is a structured activity designed to facilitate discussion among key stakeholders regarding their roles and responsibilities during a cybersecurity incident. These exercises often involve a scenario that simulates a cyber-attack or data breach, allowing participants to work through the incident in a low-pressure environment. The focus is on strategic decision-making, communication, and coordination among team members rather than on technical execution. Participants may include IT staff, management, legal, and public relations teams, among others, to ensure a comprehensive approach to incident response. The goal is to identify gaps in preparedness, improve response plans, and enhance overall resilience against cyber threats. By conducting tabletop exercises regularly, organizations can foster a culture of readiness and ensure that all team members understand their roles in the event of a real incident. These exercises can also facilitate learning and improvement by analyzing the responses and outcomes following the simulated scenarios.

Examples

  • Conducting a simulated ransomware attack to assess incident response procedures.
  • Role-playing a data breach scenario to improve communication and coordination among departments.

Additional Information

  • Tabletop exercises can be tailored to specific threats relevant to the organization.
  • They are often facilitated by external experts to provide an objective perspective.
