Virus

A type of malicious software that attaches itself to legitimate programs or files and can replicate itself to spread to other systems.

Description

In the context of cybersecurity, a virus is a form of malware that, when executed, replicates by inserting copies of itself into other computer programs, data files, or the boot sector of the hard drive. Viruses can be spread through various means, such as email attachments, downloaded files, or infected USB drives. Once a virus has infected a system, it can perform a variety of harmful actions, including corrupting or deleting data, stealing sensitive information, or using system resources for malicious purposes. Viruses often require human action to propagate, such as opening an infected file or program. They can be particularly damaging as they can cause system crashes, data loss, and significant downtime for individuals and organizations alike. Cybersecurity strategies against viruses include the use of antivirus software, regular system updates, and safe browsing practices to minimize the risk of infection.

Examples

  • The ILOVEYOU virus, which spread through email in 2000 and caused significant damage worldwide.
  • The Melissa virus, which also spread via email and was one of the first to use social engineering tactics to propagate.

Additional Information

  • Viruses are different from worms, which can spread independently without human action.
  • Regularly updating antivirus software and systems can help defend against virus infections.
