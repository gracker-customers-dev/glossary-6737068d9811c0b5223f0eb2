White team

A group responsible for overseeing and managing the rules of engagement during cybersecurity exercises and assessments.

Description

In the context of cybersecurity, the White team refers to a group of individuals who oversee and manage cybersecurity exercises, such as penetration tests or red team-blue team exercises. Their primary role is to ensure that the exercise runs smoothly, fairly, and according to established rules. They act as neutral parties who monitor interactions between the attacking (red team) and defending (blue team) groups, enforcing the engagement protocols and ensuring that all participants adhere to the agreed-upon guidelines. The White team is also responsible for evaluating the effectiveness of the exercise, providing feedback, and compiling reports based on observations. This group may include cybersecurity experts, compliance officers, and organizational stakeholders who ensure that the exercise meets its objectives while minimizing risks to the organization's systems and data. The White team plays a critical role in fostering an environment of learning and improvement in cybersecurity practices.

Examples

  • White team members might set the rules for a capture-the-flag (CTF) competition.
  • In a red team vs. blue team exercise, the White team monitors the engagement to ensure compliance with the rules.

Additional Information

  • The White team can include compliance officers, security analysts, and subject matter experts.
  • They often provide a debriefing session after exercises to discuss findings and areas for improvement.
